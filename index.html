The Ellis Act and San Francisco

by @DanGrover for KQED's The Lowdown


As real estate prices soar in San Francisco, landlords have turned to exploiting a state law called the Ellis Act. The law allows them to evict all tenants in a building provided the building is kept empty for five years or else rented at the old price. Landlords often do this because selling the building has become more profitable than continuing to rent it, and empty buildings typically sell for more.Read full article →
